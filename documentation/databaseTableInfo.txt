Defining the tables in the database
    -updated 7/17/13 by nik

Item(story) table
 -    wp_stories (renamed from wp_items)
   .	ID (unique)
   .	name 
   .	priority
   .	description
   .	owner_ID ( working) can be NULL
   .	container_ID
   . 	status
   .	points
   .	sprint_ID
   .    proj_ID
   .    type

Tasks table (subtasks for each item)
 -    wp_tasks
   .    ID
   .    item_ID
   .    name
   .    description
   .    status

Workers table (who's working on each item)
 -    wp_workers 
   .    ID
   .    item_ID
   .    user_ID

Sprint table
 -	wp_sprint
   .	ID
   .    proj_ID //ADDed
   .	start_date
   .	end_date
   .    completed_date
   .	description
WP-users
 -	wp_users
   .	ID
   .	user_nicename
   .	user_email
   .    site_role
   .    locked
WP-roles
 -  wp_roles
   .    ID
   .    proj_ID
   .    user_ID
   .    role
WP-proj
 -  wp_proj
   .    ID
   .    owner_ID
   .    name
   .    description
The panes on the page will be known as containers
